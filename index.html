    • What is the NFT Scam Log?

    The NFT Scam Log is a website that archives the recent rise of the NFT-metaverse industry. While making a business surrounding the NFT technology isn’t harmful, the more popular it becomes, the more people it attracts. By gaining popularity, many people take advantage of this and find ways to scam others for money.

    Why though is it a scam if a company or a person drops an NFT album and sells it to the public? Well, that by itself isn’t a scam, what makes it a scam is their promises. Usually, companies will promise people that what they are doing is “a long time investment” or that what they will deliver is a far more than just overpriced images of art NFTs. We will notice that companies promise the creation of games, or metaverses from these NFTs, lying to people and often delivering nothing or publish unpolished products, which are not properly functionable or even good looking.

    What is an NFT?

    Many might be wondering what is an NFT, or might be confused on what is this website, what am I talking about even? Of course you can google NFTs and look it up on Wikipedia by yourself, or if you are here, you must probably know what an NFT is, an by this point, why are you reading this section at all?

    To put it simply, and in a very short way, NFTs are digital tokes, also called (non-fungible token), In which any person can buy on the blockchain. Types of NFT data units may be associated with digital files such as photos, videos, and audio. Because each token is uniquely identifiable, NFTs differ from blockchain cryptocurrencies, such as Bitcoin. NFT ledgers claim to provide a public certificate of authenticity or proof of ownership, but the legal rights conveyed by an NFT can be uncertain. NFTs do not restrict the sharing or copying of the underlying digital files, do not necessarily convey the copyright of the digital files, and do not prevent the creation of NFTs with identical associated files.
